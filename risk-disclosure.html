HIGH RISK INVESTMENT WARNING

Trading foreign exchange (forex) on margin carries a high level of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. The high degree of leverage can work against you as well as for you. Before deciding to trade forex, you should carefully consider your investment objectives, level of experience, and risk appetite. The possibility exists that you could sustain a loss of some or all of your initial investment, and therefore you should not invest money that you cannot afford to lose.

1. General Risk of Forex Trading

Foreign exchange trading involves the simultaneous buying and selling of currencies. This market operates 24 hours a day, 5 days a week, and is one of the largest and most liquid financial markets in the world. However, this also means it carries significant risks:

  • Market Risk: Currency prices can move rapidly in either direction due to economic events, geopolitical developments, central bank decisions, and countless other factors that are impossible to predict with certainty.
  • Leverage Risk: Forex trading typically involves leverage, meaning you can control a large position with a relatively small amount of capital. While leverage can amplify profits, it equally amplifies losses. You can lose more than your initial deposit.
  • Liquidity Risk: During periods of extreme market volatility, low liquidity, or around major news events, it may not be possible to execute orders at desired prices, leading to slippage.
  • Gap Risk: Prices can "gap" over weekends or during periods of extreme volatility, potentially triggering losses beyond your stop-loss levels.
  • Interest Rate Risk: Changes in interest rates by central banks can cause significant currency movements.
  • Country & Political Risk: Political instability, economic sanctions, or sudden regulatory changes in any country can dramatically affect currency valuations.

2. Risks Specific to Automated Trading

In addition to the general risks of forex trading, the use of automated trading software such as Omega Titan carries additional specific risks:

2.1 Software Risk

  • No software is free from bugs or errors. Despite thorough testing, unforeseen issues may cause the Software to behave unexpectedly.
  • Software updates may introduce new bugs or change trading behavior.
  • The Software may not perform as expected in all market conditions, particularly conditions not encountered during backtesting.

2.2 Technology & Infrastructure Risk

  • Server failures: Your VPS or local machine may experience outages, preventing the Software from executing or managing trades.
  • Network disruptions: Internet connectivity issues between your server and the broker's API can prevent order execution.
  • API failures: The OANDA API (or any broker API) may experience downtime, rate limiting, or connectivity issues.
  • Power outages: Hardware failures or power losses can interrupt trading operations.
  • Data feed errors: Incorrect or delayed price data from the broker can lead to erroneous trading decisions.

2.3 Execution Risk

  • Slippage: Orders may be filled at prices different from the expected price, especially during high volatility.
  • Requotes: The broker may reject orders and offer different prices.
  • Spread widening: During news events or low liquidity periods, spreads can widen dramatically, increasing trading costs.
  • Order rejection: Broker may reject orders due to insufficient margin, account restrictions, or technical issues.

2.4 Over-Optimization Risk

  • Trading algorithms can be "over-fitted" to historical data, performing well in backtests but poorly in live trading.
  • Market regimes change over time. Strategies that worked historically may become less effective or unprofitable.
  • There is no guarantee that any algorithm will continue to generate the same results as its backtested performance.

3. Backtested Performance Disclaimer

HYPOTHETICAL PERFORMANCE RESULTS HAVE MANY INHERENT LIMITATIONS. No representation is being made that any account will or is likely to achieve profits or losses similar to those shown in any backtested results.

Backtested results presented on our website or in any materials have the following limitations:

  • Hindsight bias: Backtests are designed with the benefit of historical data. They reflect what WOULD have happened, not what DID happen in real-time.
  • No emotional factor: Backtests cannot account for the psychological aspects of trading, including the impact of losses on decision-making.
  • Execution assumptions: Backtests assume perfect execution at historical prices. Real execution involves slippage, requotes, and partial fills.
  • Spread assumptions: While our backtests include spread costs, actual spreads vary by time of day, market conditions, and broker.
  • Survivorship bias: Parameters were selected in part based on which performed well historically. This does not guarantee future performance.
  • Market regime changes: Historical market conditions may not repeat. Economic events, regulatory changes, and shifts in market structure can fundamentally alter how markets behave.
  • Data quality: Backtests rely on historical data from OANDA's practice API. This data may contain errors, gaps, or inaccuracies that affect results.
PAST PERFORMANCE IS NOT INDICATIVE OF FUTURE RESULTS. There is no guarantee that any trading strategy, including the strategies employed by Omega Titan, will be profitable. You should expect to experience losses, including periods of sustained drawdown.

4. Financial Suitability

Before using Omega Titan or engaging in forex trading, you should honestly assess whether it is appropriate for your financial situation:

  • Only trade with risk capital: Use only funds that you can afford to lose entirely without affecting your lifestyle, financial obligations, or well-being.
  • Emergency funds are NOT risk capital: Never trade with money earmarked for rent, mortgage, bills, education, healthcare, or emergency savings.
  • Understand leverage: If you do not fully understand how leverage works, do not use leveraged products.
  • Consider professional advice: If you are unsure whether forex trading is appropriate for you, consult a qualified independent financial advisor before proceeding.
  • Tax implications: Trading profits may be subject to capital gains tax or other taxes in your jurisdiction. You are responsible for understanding and complying with your local tax obligations.
